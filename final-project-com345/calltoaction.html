Call to Action

What can we do to help?

The importance of this issue truly cannot be understated. Sports broadcasting is incredibly male-dominanted, leading the small portion of women in the field to be hypersexualized and deemed incompetent by their male peers. In addition, without women in the field, it is unlikely that there will be a push to highlight women's sports to the same degree as men's, continuing trends that ignore womens' acomplishments in all aspects of athelics, and to disregard their own struggles and hard work.

There are a multitude of ways you can get involved. The first is simple -- watch female broadcasters. Since broadcasting is an industry built on views and followship, supporting women in broadcasting is easily achieveable simply by interactng with their content. If enough eyes are engaged with their pieces, it showcases the need for a diverse workplace, and the importance their perspective has on the industry.

Secondly, there is always a place for advocacy when discussing women's issues. When watching sports with family or friends over the holidays, call out dispariging remarks made when female broadcasters are on screen. While uncomfortable at least, it's important to educate and share the issues with whomever is around you.

Finally, forward information about the Association of Women in Sports Media, the organization we have chosen to highlight. AWSM not only has a variety of membership benefits, but they actively mentor members and provide a variety of internships and jobs for women in sports broadcasting. In order for them to continue their advocacy work, consider donating to their organization to keep such opportunities alive.
